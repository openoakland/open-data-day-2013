February 23, 2013, Oakland

81st Avenue Oakland Public Library. Everyone welcome.

Oaklanders gathering to write apps, liberate data, create visualizations, and publish analyses using open public data to show support for and encourage the adoption of open data policies by organizations in Oakland.

Register now!

#openoakland

Who Should Come?

Developers! We need computer cowboys and cowgirls like yourself to wrangle data into something useful. That means visualization, notification, integration, etc., all in the name of doing something crazy and fantastic.

Designers: We need people like you to make the everything look amazing, feel intuitive, and have a smooth user experience. The best application in the world that no one can use... isn't much use! You know the drill.

Librarians: I heard you folks like books and eat catalogs of data for breakfast. You beautiful people are going to scour the earth for interesting data, help the rest of us figure out what’s important, and generally be useful.

Statisticians and GIS folk: YES! YOU ARE SO NEEDED. Seriously. While we can find it, blow it up, calculate it, and make it look pretty, we needs us some mean number crunchin’ to present meaningful visualizations. Join up.

Citizens: We need you the most. If it weren’t for you, this whole thing wouldn’t be happening. We need ideas, cheerleaders, and friends to spread the word.

Do you have ideas, a data project, an idea for an app and the data to make it happen? Tell us!
